Meta Platforms Inc. Q4 2025 Earnings Call Summary (January 28, 2026)

Meta reported Q4 2025 revenue of $59.9 billion, beating analyst estimates of $58.4 billion. Earnings per share came in at $8.88, significantly above the $8.19 consensus. The stock surged over 10% in after-hours trading on the results.

CEO Mark Zuckerberg led the call with an unusually aggressive vision statement about AI. He announced that Meta's 2026 capital expenditure would be $115 billion to $135 billion, up from $72.2 billion in 2025 — representing up to an 87% increase. He described this as "front-loading" compute capacity to win what he characterized as a race toward superintelligence.

Advertising revenue showed strong momentum across all platforms, with Instagram Reels and AI-powered ad targeting driving improved engagement and advertiser ROI. Family of Apps daily active people reached 3.4 billion.

Zuckerberg stated: "I believe we will look back on this period as one where the companies that invested aggressively in AI infrastructure gained a permanent advantage. I would rather over-invest and be right than under-invest and be wrong."

The Q1 2026 revenue guidance of $53.5 billion to $56.5 billion exceeded the $51.3 billion consensus estimate, further bolstering confidence in the near-term trajectory.

However, the massive CapEx guidance prompted significant pushback during the Q&A session. Multiple analysts questioned the return on investment timeline and whether the spending was disciplined or speculative. Some drew parallels to the metaverse spending backlash of 2022-2023.

Reality Labs continued to lose money at a rate of approximately $5 billion per quarter, though Zuckerberg emphasized that the division's work on AI glasses and mixed reality was converging with the broader AI strategy.

The analyst consensus after the call was split: near-term fundamentals were undeniably strong, but the long-term risk profile had increased significantly due to the spending commitments.
